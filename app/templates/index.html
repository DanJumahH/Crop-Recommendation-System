About the Project

Model performance, methodology
and research findings.

CropIQ is a final year project by Group 18, Department of Computer Science, Kwara State University, Malete. The system uses XGBoost machine learning trained on an Ethiopian agricultural dataset (Alemu, 2024) augmented with real topographic features derived from SRTM/NASADEM elevation data and seasonal climate profiles from Open-Meteo ERA5 reanalysis.

Results & Analysis

Figure 1 — Crop class distribution before balancing. Teff dominates with 1,260 records while Red Pepper has only 29, yielding a 43.4:1 imbalance ratio. BorderlineSMOTE was applied to bring minority classes to 500 training samples.

Figure 2 — Training set class distribution before and after BorderlineSMOTE (target = 500). Majority classes (Teff, Maize, Wheat, Barley) are left untouched; all minority classes are up-sampled to 500 synthetic samples.

Figure 3 — Descriptive statistics of key soil nutrient features. High standard deviations in K (202.25) and P (34.14) indicate wide variability across Ethiopian highland zones. Outlier capping at the 1st–99th percentile was applied prior to training.

Figure 4 — Test set accuracy comparison across all three models. XGBoost achieved the highest accuracy at 64.22%, outperforming both Random Forest (56.78%) and the soft ensemble combination (63.94%), making it the selected deployment model.

Figure 5 — Per-class accuracy for XGBoost. Red Pepper (98.3%) and Potato (91.7%) achieved the highest scores. Bean (25.0%) was the most challenging class, likely due to overlapping soil and climate profiles with Barley and Wheat.

Figure 6 — Confusion matrix for XGBoost on the test set (n=1,062). The most common misclassification is Teff predicted as Maize (36 cases) and Wheat predicted as Barley (26 cases), reflecting the climate profile overlap between these highland cereals.

Figure 7 — Top 20 XGBoost feature importances by gain. Winter precipitation (PRECTOTCORR-W, 0.0688) and wind direction (WD10M, 0.0614) are the strongest predictors. Engineered features T_range_Au and T_range_Su both appear in the top 15, validating the feature engineering step.

Figure 8 — Per-class Precision, Recall and F1-Score. The weighted F1 average is 64.1% (dashed line). Red Pepper, Potato and Dagussa exceed 84% F1, while Bean (30.9%) and Barley (43.5%) remain below average due to class overlap with similar highland crops.
